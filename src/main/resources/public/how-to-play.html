How To Play 'Kalaha'

The Kalaha board is made up of two rows of six pits (holes) each. Six "stones" are placed in each of the 12 pits. Each player has a "Kalaha" (a store) to his/her right side of the Kalaha board.

Objective

The objective of the game is to collect the most stones by the end of the game.

Game Play

1. The game begins with one player picking up all of the stones in any one of the pits on his/her side.

2. Moving counter-clockwise, the player deposits one of the stones in each Kalaha until the stones run out.

3. If you run into your own Kalaha, deposit one piece in it. If you run into your opponent's Kalaha, skip it and continue moving to the next Kalaha.

4. If the last piece you drop is in your own Kalaha, you take another turn.

5. If the last piece you drop is in an empty Kalaha on your side, you capture that piece and any stones in the Kalaha directly opposite.

6. Always place all captured stones in your Kalaha.

7. The game ends when all six pits on one side of the Kalaha board are empty.

8. The player who still has stones on his/her side of the board when the game ends captures all of those stones.

9. Count all the stones in each Kalaha. The winner is the player with the most stones.
